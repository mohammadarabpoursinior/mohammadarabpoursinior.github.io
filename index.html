cDC1-Targeted Mucosal Vaccine

Our lead platform combines a cholera toxin A1 (CTA1) adjuvant with a single-chain anti-CD103 antibody, delivering tumor antigen and adjuvant simultaneously to migratory CD103⁺ cDC1 following intranasal administration.

This co-delivery strategy generates robust antigen-specific CD8⁺ T-cell responses alongside Th17-skewed CD4⁺ immunity — with proven efficacy in melanoma and breast cancer metastasis models.

Bispecific T-Cell Engager (ImmTAC)

Our TCR-based bispecific engager platform redirects non-disease-specific T cells toward tumor cells presenting HLA-restricted neoantigens. Applied to NPM1-mutated AML — a major subtype representing 20-30% of AML cases.

The scTCR-anti-CD3 format bridges peptide-HLA on malignant cells to polyclonal effector T cells, enabling precision killing without CAR-T manufacturing complexity.

HLA-Restricted Neoantigen TargetingPeptide-MHC complex recognition via scTCR domain
Off-the-Shelf PotentialNo patient-specific T-cell manufacturing required
AML ValidationNPM1-mutant specific binding confirmed in PBMC assays

CRISPR TCR/CAR Knock-In

CRISPR-Cas9-mediated precision insertion of therapeutic TCR or CAR constructs into the TRAC locus — replacing random viral integration with controlled, physiological expression for safer, more potent cell therapies.

Our optimization pipeline addresses key barriers — DNA toxicity, NHEJ events, and patient variability — to achieve clinical-grade knock-in efficiency.
